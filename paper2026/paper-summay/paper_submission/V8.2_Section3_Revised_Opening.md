## OPTION A: Bridge + Forward Momentum (Recommended) - NO SECTION REFERENCES

Having established the need for dynamic personality modeling, theoretically grounded regulation, and validated evaluation frameworks, this section describes how these innovations are operationalized and tested. We adopt a controlled simulation-based methodology designed to isolate the contribution of personality-adaptive mechanisms in conversational AI. The proposed system integrates real-time Big Five personality trait inference with behavior regulation grounded in the Zurich Model of Social Motivation, allowing the assistant to adapt its interaction style across dialogue turns in response to stable personality characteristics. To distinguish the effects of personality-driven adaptation from general conversational competence, the regulated system is compared against a non-adaptive baseline that provides emotionally supportive but personality-agnostic responses. All interactions employ standardized simulated personas and a fixed dialogue structure to ensure experimental control and reproducibility.

The methodological framework is organized around three core objectives corresponding to the gaps identified above. First, we examine whether stable personality traits can be accurately inferred from conversational cues in real time within multi-turn interactions, addressing the static vs. dynamic modeling gap. Second, we evaluate whether theory-driven personality-adaptive regulation produces measurable improvements in addressing personality-specific user needs beyond generic supportive responses, addressing the theoretical gap. Third, we assess the validity of an LLM-based evaluation framework by benchmarking its judgments against independent human expert ratings, thereby establishing a scalable yet reliable approach to evaluating conversational quality and personalization. Together, these objectives define a coherent methodological pathway for evaluating personality-adaptive conversational AI and provide a foundation for subsequent empirical extensions beyond simulation-based research.
