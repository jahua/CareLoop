Dear Editors,

Please find our manuscript entitled “Personality-Aware Behavior Regulation Improves Emotional Support Quality in Chatbots: A Simulation Study for Healthcare Settings,” submitted for consideration in the Special Issue “Artificial Intelligence in Healthcare: Opportunities and Challenges.”

This work presents a modular, transparent approach to real-time personality detection (Big Five) and motivationally grounded behavior regulation (Zurich Model) for conversational agents supporting older adults at risk of loneliness. In controlled simulations across two extreme personality profiles, regulated assistants improved shared evaluation criteria by approximately 34% over non-adaptive baselines, with consistent gains in tone, coherence, and personality-need alignment.

The manuscript addresses both opportunities and challenges relevant to the special issue—governance and safety, equity and bias, clinical integration, privacy and consent, outcomes evaluation, and technical robustness—offering a practical architecture that is auditable and maintainable.

All authors approve this submission and confirm that the manuscript is original, not under review elsewhere, and complies with ethical standards (no human subjects). We believe this work will be of interest to readers developing and deploying supportive AI companions in healthcare contexts.
