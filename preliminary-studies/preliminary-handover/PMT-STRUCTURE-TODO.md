### PMT Structure Checklist (To-Do with acceptance criteria)

- **Background**
  - Gather problem context (elder-care, loneliness, gap) from `Samuel_paper /Samuel-Devdas_Master-Thesis-clean.md` and literature.
  - Acceptance: ≤1.5 pages; ≥3 citations; gap in 1 paragraph.

- **Topic Definition**
  - Define "personality-adaptive emotional support chatbot" and Zurich Model alignment.
  - Acceptance: 3–5 crisp definitions with scope boundaries.

- **Research Questions**
  - Primary RQ + 3–5 sub-RQs extending Samuel’s work to framework/testing.
  - Acceptance: Testable; mapped to methodology.

- **Methodology**
  - Describe detection→regulation pipeline, evaluation matrix, and automation plan.
  - Acceptance: Inputs/outputs, metrics, and procedures specified.

- **Underlying Data Sources**
  - List simulated dialogues, prompt libraries, optional public datasets; licensing.
  - Acceptance: Each source has path, license, usage, preprocessing.

- **Technology, Software, and Applications**
  - Choose LLM(s), memory, orchestration (e.g., LangGraph), vector DB, eval stack.
  - Acceptance: Justification table + versioned tools.

- **Annotated Disposition**
  - Outline PMT and thesis sections with 1–2 sentence annotations.
  - Acceptance: All sections covered; clear purpose.

- **Project Risks**
  - 8–12 risks (data, eval bias, tool limits, reproducibility, ethics) with mitigations.
  - Acceptance: Risk matrix with likelihood/impact/owner.

- **Work and Research Plan**
  - Timeline and milestones for paper summary, framework selection, prototype, eval, PMT, thesis.
  - Acceptance: Gantt-style table with deliverables and owners.

- **References**
  - Consolidate citations (APA/IEEE) including Samuel’s thesis and new sources.
  - Acceptance: Consistent style, no broken refs.

- **Appendix**
  - Prompts, evaluation matrices, diagrams, configs, versions.
  - Acceptance: Artifacts reproducible and consistent with text.
