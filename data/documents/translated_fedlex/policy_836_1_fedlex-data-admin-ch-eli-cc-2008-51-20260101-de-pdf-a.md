# 2. Chapter: General Provisions

## Article 2 Definition and Purpose of Family Allowances

Family Allowances

Family allowances are one-time or periodic cash benefits that are designed to partially offset the financial burden of having one or more children.

## Article 3 Types of Family Allowances; Competences of the Cantons

1The family allowances under this Act include:

a.the child allowance: it is paid from the month of the child's birth until the end of the month in which the child reaches the age of 16; if the child is already entitled to an apprenticeship allowance before reaching the age of 16, this is paid instead of the child allowance; if the child is unable to work (Art. 7 ATSG11), the child allowance is paid until the end of the month in which they reach the age of 20;b.the apprenticeship allowance: it is paid from the month in which the child begins post-compulsory education, but no earlier than the month in which they reach the age of 15; if the child is still attending compulsory school after reaching the age of 16, the apprenticeship allowance is paid from the following month; the apprenticeship allowance is granted until the completion of the child's education, but no later than the end of the month in which they reach the age of 25.

2The cantons may provide for higher minimum amounts for child and apprenticeship allowances in their family allowance ordinances. The provisions of this Act also apply to these family allowances. Other benefits must be regulated and financed outside the family allowance ordinances. Benefits provided for in collective or individual labour agreements or other regulations are not considered family allowances within the meaning of this Act.

3The birth allowance is paid for each child born alive or after at least 23 weeks of pregnancy. The Federal Council may set further conditions. The adoption allowance is paid for each minor child taken into care for later adoption. There is no entitlement to adoption of a child under Article 264c of

# 4.Chapter: Legal Proceedings and Penal Provisions

## Art.22 Special Features of Legal Proceedings

In deviation from Article 58 paragraphs 1 and 2 ATSG, the Insurance Court of the canton whose Family Allowances Ordinance is applicable decides on complaints against decisions of the Family Equalization Funds.

## Art.23 Penal Provisions

Articles 87–91 AHVG are applicable to persons who violate the provisions of this Act in the manner described in these provisions.
