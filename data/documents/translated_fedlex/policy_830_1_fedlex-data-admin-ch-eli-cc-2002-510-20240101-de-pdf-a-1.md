# Chapter 1: Scope of Application

Art. 1 Purpose and Subject Matter

This Act coordinates federal social insurance law by:

a. defining principles, terms, and institutions of social insurance law;
b. establishing a uniform social insurance procedure and regulating legal remedies;
c. harmonizing benefits; d. regulating the recourse of social insurances to third parties

Art. 2 Scope of Application and Relationship to Individual Social Insurance Acts

The provisions of this Act are applicable to social insurances regulated by federal law, when and to the extent that the individual social insurance acts provide for it.

### Article 23: Waiver of Benefits

1. The entitled person may waive insurance benefits. They may revoke the waiver at any time for the future. Waiver and revocation must be in writing.

2. Waiver and revocation are void if the interests worthy of protection of other persons, insurances, or welfare offices are affected or if the intention is to circumvent statutory provisions.

3. The insurer must confirm the waiver and revocation in writing to the entitled person. In the confirmation, the subject, scope, and consequences of the waiver and revocation must be specified.

### Article 24: Extinguishing of Claims

1. The claim to outstanding benefits or contributions extinguishes five years after the end of the month for which the benefit is due, and five years after the end of the calendar year for which the contribution is owed.

2. If a contributory person has evaded their obligations by a criminal act for which the criminal law sets a longer statute of limitations, this period applies to the extinguishing of the contribution claim.

### Article 25: Reimbursement

1. Benefits improperly received must be reimbursed. Whoever has received benefits in good faith does not have to reimburse them if there is a significant hardship.

2. The claim for reimbursement extinguishes three years after the insurance organization has become aware of it, at the latest five years after the payment of the individual benefit. If the claim for reimbursement is derived from a criminal act for which the criminal law provides a longer statute of limitations, this period applies.

3. Overpaid contributions can be claimed back. The claim extinguishes one year after the contributor becomes aware of their excessive payments, at the latest five years after the calendar year in which the contributions were paid.

# Art.61Procedural Rules

830.1

The procedure before the cantonal insurance court is determined, with the reservation of Article 1 Paragraph 3 of the Administrative Procedure Act of December 20, 1968, according to cantonal law. It must meet the following requirements:

$a.^{47}$The procedure must be simple, fast, and generally public.

b.The appeal must contain a concise presentation of the facts, a legal claim, and a brief justification. If it does not meet these requirements, the insurance court sets an appropriate deadline for the appellant to improve and threatens that otherwise the appeal will not be considered.
c.The insurance court, with the participation of the parties, establishes the facts relevant to the decision; it takes the necessary evidence and is free in the assessment of evidence.
d.The insurance court is not bound by the claims of the parties. It can change a ruling or an objection decision to the disadvantage of the appellant, or grant more than they have claimed, provided that the parties are given the opportunity to comment and to withdraw the appeal beforehand.
e.If circumstances justify it, the parties can be summoned to a hearing.
f.The right to be represented must be guaranteed. Where circumstances justify it, free legal assistance is granted to the appellant. fbis.48In disputes over benefits, the procedure is subject to fees if this is provided for in the respective individual act; if the individual act does not provide for fees in such disputes, the court can impose court costs on a party that has acted intentionally or negligently.
g.The prevailing appellant is entitled to compensation for party costs. These are determined by the insurance court and are assessed without regard to the dispute value according to the significance of the dispute and the difficulty of the procedure.
h.Decisions are made in writing, with a justification and a notice of legal remedies, as well as the names of the members of the insurance court.
i.The possibility of revising decisions due to the discovery of new facts or evidence or due to the influence of crime or offense must be guaranteed.
