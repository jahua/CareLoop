## Section 2: Entitlement to Supplementary Benefits

Art. 4 General Conditions

1Persons with residence and habitual abode (Art. 13 ATSG) in Switzerland are entitled to supplementary benefits if they:

a.receive an old-age pension (AHV) under the Old Age and Survivors' Insurance (OASI);

b.are entitled to a survivors' pension under AHV as long as they have not reached the reference age according to Article 21 Paragraph 1 of the Federal Act of December 20, 1946, on Old Age and Survivors' Insurance (AHVG);

c.are entitled to a survivors' pension under Article 24b AHVG instead of an old-age pension;

d.are entitled to a survivors' pension under AHV;

e.would be entitled to a pension under AHV if:

1.they met the minimum insurance period according to Article 29 Paragraph 1 AHVG, or
2.the deceased person would have met the minimum insurance period and the surviving spouse has not yet reached the reference age according to Article 21 AHVG;

f.are entitled to a pension or helplessness allowance (HE) under disability insurance (IV) or have received a daily allowance (Taggeld) under IV for at least six months without interruption; or

g.would be entitled to a pension under IV if they met the minimum insurance period according to Article 36 Paragraph 1 of the Federal Act of June 19, 1959, on disability insurance.

2Separated spouses and divorced persons with residence and habitual abode (Art. 13 ATSG) in Switzerland are also entitled to supplementary benefits if they receive an additional pension under AHV or IV.

3The habitual residence in Switzerland according to Paragraph 1 is considered interrupted if a person:

a.stays abroad for more than three months without interruption; or b.stays abroad for more than three months in total within a calendar year.15

4The Federal Council determines the point in time for the suspension and resumption of benefits, as well as the cases in which the habitual residence in Switzerland is not considered interrupted during a stay abroad of up to one year.

Art. 5 Additional Conditions for Foreigners

1Foreigners are only entitled to supplementary benefits if they are lawfully residing in Switzerland. They must also have been residing in Switzerland without interruption for ten years immediately before the time when the supplementary benefit is claimed.

2For refugees and stateless persons, the waiting period is five years.

b.five years for persons who, as long as they have not reached the reference age according to Article 21 Paragraph 1 AHVG, would be entitled to a survivors' pension under AHV if the deceased person had met the minimum insurance period according to Article 29 Paragraph 1 AHVG at the time of their death;

## Art. 26a93 Disclosure of Data to the Migration Authorities

For the examination of the conditions for family reunification and the right to residence, the bodies responsible for the determination and payment of supplementary benefits according to Article 97 paragraph 3 letter d of the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act of 16 December 2005 and, in derogation from Article 33 ATSG95, the migration authorities shall be informed without request of the receipt of an annual supplementary benefit according to Article 3 paragraph 1 letter a by foreign nationals. ## Art. 26b96 EL-Information System

The Central Compensation Office according to Article 71 AHVG97 operates an information system for the processing of data in the field of supplementary benefits (EL-Information System), in particular to create transparency about received supplementary benefits and to support the bodies according to Article 21 paragraph 2 in the enforcement of this Act.
