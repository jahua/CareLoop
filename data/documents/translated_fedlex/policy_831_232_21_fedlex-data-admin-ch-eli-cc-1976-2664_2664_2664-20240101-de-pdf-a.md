# 14.06Assistance dogs

Old-age, Survivors and Disability Insurance

14.06.1Mobility assistance dog for physically disabled persons from the age of 16,

provided the suitability of the insured person as an assistance dog handler is proven and they can live more independently at home thanks to this help. The claim only exists for severely physically disabled persons who receive compensation for helplessness of at least minor degree with proven need for assistance in at least two of the following categories: mobility/social contacts; getting up/sitting down/sitting;

The provision site of the mobility assistance dog must be certified by the organization Assistance Dogs International (ADI). The insurance reimburses a lump sum contribution of 20280 Swiss francs at the time of provision of the assistance dog. The contribution is composed as follows: 15000 Swiss francs for the purchase costs and 5280 Swiss francs for food and veterinary costs. The benefit can be claimed at most every eight years, but only once for each dog.

14.06.2Epilepsy warning dog for children from 4 years of age and for adults,

provided the suitability of the insured person or a holder or a holder of parental care as a warning dog handler is confirmed by the provision site. The claim only exists if epilepsy is medically diagnosed. Adults must also achieve an integration goal according to Article 21 Paragraphs 1 and 2 IVG31 thanks to the dog.

The provision site of the epilepsy warning dog must be certified by the organization Assistance Dogs International (ADI). The insurance reimburses a lump sum contribution of 14280 Swiss francs. The contribution is composed as follows: 9000 Swiss francs for the purchase costs and 5280 Swiss francs for food and veterinary costs. The benefit can be claimed at most every eight years, but only once for each dog.
