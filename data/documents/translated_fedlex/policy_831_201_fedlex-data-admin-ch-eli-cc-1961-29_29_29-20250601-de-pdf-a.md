1 After the completion of compulsory schooling, the following are considered first vocational training:

- a. vocational basic training according to the Vocational Training Act of December 13, 2002 (VBG);

## Art.22quater140 Compensation for care costs

1. Care costs include, in particular, compensation for:

   a. costs for meals outside the home for the persons mentioned in Article 11a Paragraph 2 IVG;
   b. travel and accommodation costs for the persons mentioned in Article 11a Paragraph 2 IVG who are cared for by third parties;
   c. wages for family or household help;
   d. costs for children's nurseries, day or after-school care or day structures;
   e. travel costs of third parties who care for the persons mentioned in Article 11a Paragraph 2 IVG in the household of the entitled person.

2. The actual costs are compensated, but at most the multiple corresponding to the number of effective integration days of 20 percent of the maximum amount of the daily allowance according to Article 24 Paragraph 1.

3. Care costs of less than 20 francs in total are not compensated.

## Art.22quinquies141 Child allowance

1. Child and training allowances in the sense of Article 22bis Paragraph 2 IVG are considered statutory supplements under federal law, cantonal law, and foreign law.

2. The compensation fund can require the insured person to prove that there is no entitlement to a statutory child or training allowance.

3. If there is an entitlement to a statutory child or training allowance and the child allowance would be higher than this, the insured person is not entitled to a child allowance.

## Art.23bis144 Integration measures abroad for compulsorily insured persons

1. If the implementation of an integration measure in Switzerland proves to be impossible, in particular because the necessary institutions or specialists are missing, the insurance covers the costs of a simple and appropriate implementation abroad.

2. The insurance covers the costs for the simple and appropriate implementation of medical measures that are urgently carried out abroad.

3. If an integration measure is carried out abroad for other significant reasons, the insurance compensates the costs up to the extent to which such services would have been provided in Switzerland.

## Art.23ter145 Integration measures abroad for voluntarily insured persons

1. The insurance covers the costs for integration measures abroad if special circumstances justify this and the measures are likely to contribute to the affected person being able to carry out gainful employment or engage in the task area again.

2. For persons under the age of 20, the insurance covers the costs for measures carried out abroad if their chances of success and the personal circumstances of the affected person justify this.
